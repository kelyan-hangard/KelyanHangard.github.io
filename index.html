Analysis of non-linear air pollution in Python and R

Utilized Python and R to perform a comprehensive spatio-temporal analysis of Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2) and fine particulate matter (PM2.5) across Europe. Science vs Art: Defying the Brain's Divisions in Python

This project aims to challenge the stereotype that artistic and scientific people are inherently different and not interested in each other's subjects. We analyzed the YouNiverse dataset (huge Youtube dataset), which includes 8.6 billion comments and 72 million video metadata files, to determine if users who engage with artistic videos also engage with scientific videos, and vice versa. We also intend to divide artistic and scientific videos into smaller domains to examine whether this pattern is influenced by the specific domain of art or science that the user is interested in. By studying user profiles and our watching/commenting behaviors on videos that do not typically align with our profiles, we seek to draw conclusions about people's interests.

Machine Learning and Deep Learning Time Serie Forecasting in Python and Matlab

In this project hosted by the Distributed Electrical Systems Laboratory (DESL), two forecasting models with different time windows were developed. They predict the electricity consumption of an EPFL building (CM) with photovoltaic panels given previous data on the building’s electrical consumption and meteorological data (irradiance and temperature). The pre-processing steps taken with the data, including handling of missing data, the feature engineering process used to generate additional relevant features, the various models designed using different ML approaches and their results are all detailed in the report. The final models chosen, artificial neural networks with hypertuned parameters, achieve 62.09% accuracy for next-day forecasting and 62.96% accuracy for intra-day forecasting. These results had been judged excellent by the DESL of EPFL.

Deep Learning Classification in Python

This project focuses on using transfer learning methods to solve an image classification problem. In particular, we use the ResNet34 and ResNet18 ConvNets to classify the images found in the Oxford-IIIT Pet Dataset. We also explore the effects of different parameters such as number of epochs and learning rates, as well as seeing how data augmentation and freezing some layers affects our results. We discovered that with transfer learning, it was possible to classify the image as a cat or a dog with an accuracy greater than 99%. Furthermore, we also managed to get an accuracy of more than 95% when trying to classify the breed of cats and dogs.

Interractive City Managment Application in C++

The project involves developing a tool in C++ for editing a two-dimensional city plan. The focus is on optimizing movements, balancing neighborhood types, and estimating infrastructure costs. The project utilizes object-oriented programming principles and incorporates modules, separation of concerns, and encapsulation. It employs geometric and graph abstractions to ensure proper distribution of neighborhoods and connections, and to evaluate the effectiveness of the plan. The program allows for file reading, saving, writing, interactive editing of nodes and links, and features a graphical user interface (GUI). The project showcases object-oriented programming skills in C++ and tackles complex constraints related to neighborhood representation and pathfinding using Dijkstra's algorithm.

Development of Laser Scanner Module for SileX System in Fusion 360 and Matlab

I actively participated in a microengineering project where I played a crucial role in developing a laser scanner module for the SileX system. The project aimed to create a laser micro-machining machine for the production of large-scale opto-mechanical microsystems in glass. The goal of the project was to implement flexible guidings to minimize energy losses caused by system friction. I utilized Matlab for calculations and optimizations, as well as Fusion CAD software for 3D printing and generating the mechanism's designs. The primary objective was to develop a dynamically balanced two-degree-of-freedom (roll and pitch) mechanism capable of rotating at frequencies up to 820 Hz. We successfully fulfilled all the specifications outlined in the project requirements.

MEMS Device for Optical Fiber Control in COMSOL and Klayout

In this MEMS (Microelectromechanical Systems) project, I contributed to the design, fabrication, evaluation, and failure analysis of a MEMS device for controlling optical fibers. The device was designed to insert or remove a micro-mirror in an X-crossing optical path. It featured lateral movement driven by voltage and could securely lock in fully inserted and fully removed positions. We began with a meticulous design phase, calculating dimensions for the actuator, restoring mechanism, and locking mechanism. Simulations using COMSOL verified the designs. Fabrication took place in clean rooms in Kista, Sweden. Subsequently, we evaluated the devices, comparing their performance with expected results and identifying any failures or areas for improvement.
